Rubric Description

This rubic contains only the observable elements.

I-A-1. Subject Matter Knowledge

  • Knowledge of the subject matter: Understanding and application of the content and structure of the discipline, prioritizing key ideas and skills and including prerequisite relationships and connections to other disciplines.
  • Knowledge of the pedagogy: Understanding and application of the pedagogies proven to be effective in teaching the given subject and grade-level.
  • Knowledge of the Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Understanding and application of the content and pedagogy of the MACF as they relate to the given subject and grade-level.
  • Learning experiences: Tasks assigned to students to develop understanding of relevant content and skills.

  • Demonstrates limited knowledge of the subject matter and/or its pedagogy; relies heavily on textbooks or resources for development of the factual content. Rarely engages students in learning experiences focused on complex knowledge or skills in the subject.

  • Demonstrates factual knowledge of subject matter and the pedagogy it requires by sometimes engaging students in learning experiences around complex knowledge and skills in the subject.

  • Demonstrates sound knowledge and understanding of the subject matter and the pedagogy it requires by consistently engaging students in learning experiences that enable them to acquire complex knowledge and skills in the subject.

  • Demonstrates expertise in subject matter and the pedagogy it requires by engaging all students in learning experiences that enable them to synthesize complex knowledge and skills in the subject. Is able to model this element.

    • When prompted, students articulate the prerequisite skills and content necessary to accomplish assigned tasks.

I-A-4. Well-Structured Lessons

  • Objectives: Explicit statement of the challenging and measureable skills that students will master by the end of the lesson.
  • Pacing & Sequence: Appropriate time is allotted to particular lesson elements. Lesson structure is clear, logical, and builds upon previous material and provides a foundation for upcoming lessons.
  • Activities: Students are asked to complete tasks that promote knowledge and skill development and require higher order thinking, through application, analysis, synthesis, or evaluation.
